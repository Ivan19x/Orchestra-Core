# Managing Bills & Utilities

When you live on your own, a new category of money appears: **recurring bills**. Power, water, gas, internet, and a creeping pile of subscriptions arrive every month whether you think about them or not. They're not huge individually, but together they're a big slice of adult spending — and because they're automatic, they're where money quietly leaks. This lesson explains how each bill actually works so you can control it, not just pay it.

## Electricity: how tokens really work

Most Kenyan homes use **prepaid tokens** (Kenya Power) — you buy units (kWh) in advance and the meter counts down. Two things surprise new payers:

**You're charged in tiers.** The more you use per month, the higher the rate per unit. As of 2026, Kenya Power's domestic bands run roughly:
- **Lifeline** (under ~30 units/month): ~KES 12/unit
- **Ordinary** (~31–100 units): ~KES 16.5/unit
- **High use** (over 100 units): ~KES 19/unit

**The price per token is more than the base rate.** On top of the base, every unit carries extra charges — a **fuel cost charge** (~KES 3/unit), a **forex charge**, small levies, and then **16% VAT** on the lot. All-in, a unit effectively costs the low-to-mid **KES 20s**, so **KES 1,000 of tokens buys only about 35–45 units** for a typical home. These add-ons change monthly with fuel and the shilling, which is why the same KES 1,000 lasts a different number of days each month.

**Control it:** switch off idle appliances (a fridge, water heater and iron are the big eaters), use energy-saving bulbs, and avoid sliding into the high-use band. Heating water and ironing are often the biggest hidden costs.

## Water

Water is usually billed by your county/water company on consumption (or a flat charge in some rentals). Watch for **leaks** (a dripping tap or running toilet can quietly double a bill), and confirm whether water is included in your rent or separate before you sign a lease.

## Cooking gas (LPG)

Gas is bought by **refilling a cylinder**. As of 2026, a **6kg refill** runs about **KES 1,100–1,390**, and a **13kg** about **KES 2,500–3,200**, varying by brand and seller. Tips:
- The **13kg works out cheaper per kilo** than the 6kg if you cook a lot — buy the size that matches your usage.
- Shop around; prices differ between brands and dealers.
- A pressure cooker and lids cut gas use noticeably.

## Internet

Home **fibre** is now competitive. As of 2026, plans range from budget options around **KES 800–2,799/month** (10–20 Mbps) up to **KES 6,000+** for fast packages — providers include Safaricom, Zuku, Faiba and others. Tips:
- **Match the speed to your need.** You rarely need the top package; a mid plan suits most homes.
- **Sharing fibre** with housemates splits the cost dramatically.
- Compare against heavy mobile-data use — for a home, fibre is usually cheaper per GB.

## Subscriptions: the silent stackers

The sneakiest bills are the small **recurring subscriptions** — streaming, music, apps, premium services. Each feels trivial (KES 300–700), but five of them is real money every month, often for things you barely use. The fix:
- **List every subscription** you pay (check your M-Pesa/bank statements for recurring charges — you'll likely find forgotten ones).
- **Cancel what you don't actively use.**
- **Share family plans** where allowed.
- **Review every few months** — subscriptions are designed to be forgotten.

## Bringing it together

- **Know each bill's mechanism** (tiered tokens, refill sizes, plan speeds) so you can choose the cheap option on purpose.
- **Budget utilities as one line** and track it — bills are predictable, so they shouldn't surprise you.
- **Audit the silent ones** (subscriptions, leaks, idle appliances) — that's where the easy savings hide.
- **Don't autopay blindly.** Automation is convenient but lets waste run unnoticed; glance at what's leaving each month.

## Key takeaways

- **Electricity tokens are tiered** and carry fuel/forex charges + VAT — KES 1,000 buys only ~35–45 units; heating and ironing are big eaters.
- **Gas:** 6kg ~KES 1,100–1,390, 13kg ~KES 2,500–3,200 — the bigger cylinder is cheaper per kilo.
- **Fibre** ranges ~KES 800–6,000+/month — match speed to need and share where possible.
- **Subscriptions silently stack** — list them, cancel the unused, review regularly.
- Treat utilities as a tracked budget line and **audit the silent leaks.**
