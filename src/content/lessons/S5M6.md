# Opening Your First Accounts

There's a moment in growing up when money stops being just coins and notes and starts being *accounts* — places that hold, move, and grow your money. Opening your first accounts is a genuine rite of passage into financial adulthood, and doing it right sets you up to save, get paid, and build from day one. The good news: it's simpler and cheaper than it looks. This lesson is a practical, step-by-step guide to the accounts every young Kenyan should have — and how to open each.

## What you'll generally need

For almost any account, have these ready:
- **A national ID** (you can get one from age 18; some youth accounts allow a birth certificate with a parent/guardian).
- **A KRA PIN** (free on iTax — useful to get early; needed for many accounts).
- **A phone number** (for M-Pesa and mobile banking).
- Sometimes a **passport photo** and proof of address.

Most accounts for young people have **low or no minimum balance and low fees** — don't let "I have no money" stop you; you can open many with almost nothing.

## 1. M-Pesa — your everyday money rail

If you don't already have it, **M-Pesa** is the foundation of daily money in Kenya:
- **What you need:** a registered Safaricom SIM and your ID.
- **How:** register at any Safaricom shop or agent (free).
- **Why first:** you'll receive money, pay bills, buy airtime, and link it to almost everything else. Learn to use it well (free Buy Goods, deposits, minimal fees — Series 4, Module 2) and to keep your PIN secret.

## 2. A bank account — to receive pay and transact

A proper bank account is needed for a formal job, larger transactions, and a card:
- **What you need:** ID, KRA PIN, a passport photo, sometimes a small opening deposit.
- **How:** apply at a branch or, increasingly, **online/via the bank's app** in minutes.
- **Choose wisely** (Series 2, Module 2): pick a **low-fee or zero-fee account with no minimum balance and a good app** — many banks have youth/student accounts built exactly for this. Avoid accounts with monthly charges you don't need.

## 3. A money market fund (MMF) — to actually grow savings

This is the account most young people *don't* know to open — and it's a game-changer:
- **What you need:** ID, KRA PIN, sometimes a small minimum (many start from ~KES 100–1,000).
- **How:** open online/app with a **CMA-licensed** fund manager; fund it via M-Pesa.
- **Why:** unlike a bank account earning ~0%, an MMF pays around **9% gross** and beats inflation — the right home for savings and your emergency fund (Series 8, Module 3).

## 4. A SACCO — to build long-term wealth and access cheap loans

As your income grows, a SACCO becomes powerful:
- **What you need:** ID, KRA PIN, the SACCO's joining/share requirements; many have **youth accounts** with low entry.
- **How:** join a **SASRA-licensed** SACCO (often open to the public now, not just employer-based).
- **Why:** strong dividends/rebates and, once you've built deposits, access to **cheap loans** (~1%/month) — the basis of many Kenyans' wealth (Series 4, Module 3).

## A sensible order for a young person

1. **M-Pesa** — you need it for everything.
2. **A low-fee bank account** — to receive pay and transact.
3. **An MMF** — the moment you have anything to save, so it actually grows.
4. **A SACCO** — as your income and discipline build, for long-term wealth and cheap credit.

You don't need all four on day one — but knowing they exist, and opening each when it fits, puts you years ahead of peers who only ever use a wallet.

## Do it safely

- **Protect your PINs and passwords** — never share them; no real institution asks for them.
- **Use only licensed providers** — CBK-supervised banks, CMA-licensed funds, SASRA-licensed SACCOs.
- **Read the fees** before opening — especially monthly charges and minimum balances.
- **Keep your details updated** and your contact secure (guard against SIM-swap, Series 4, Module 2).

## Key takeaways

- Opening your first accounts is a key step into financial adulthood — and it's cheap and simple.
- Have your **ID, KRA PIN and phone number** ready; most youth accounts have **low/no fees and minimums.**
- The core four: **M-Pesa** (daily rail), a **low-fee bank account** (to get paid), an **MMF** (to grow savings), and a **SACCO** (long-term wealth + cheap loans).
- Open them in that **order, as each fits** your stage — you don't need all at once.
- Use only **licensed providers**, guard your **PINs**, and check **fees** first.
