# Understanding Inflation & the Cost of Living

Every Kenyan feels it: the KES 100 that filled a bag of shopping a few years ago barely covers a few items today. That slow, relentless rise in prices is **inflation** — and it's one of the most important forces in your financial life, precisely because it works invisibly. Money left "safe" in cash is actually shrinking every year. Understanding inflation is what turns "I should save" into "I should save *somewhere that beats inflation*." This lesson explains it and shows how to protect yourself.

## What inflation actually is

**Inflation is the rate at which prices rise over time** — which is the same as saying the *purchasing power* of your money falls. If inflation is 7%, then on average something that cost KES 100 last year costs about KES 107 this year. Your shilling buys a little less than it used to.

In Kenya, inflation is measured by **KNBS** through the **Consumer Price Index (CPI)** — they track the price of a fixed "basket" of about 330 goods and services that households actually buy (food, transport, housing, etc.) and measure how that total changes month to month. Food carries the biggest weight (~32%), which is why food-price swings drive much of the headline number.

As of mid-2026, Kenyan inflation was around **6.7%** — within the **CBK's target band of 5% ±2.5%** (i.e. 2.5%–7.5%). The CBK uses tools like the Central Bank Rate to try to keep it in that band.

## How inflation erodes your savings (the silent tax)

Here's the part that catches careful people. Money sitting in cash or a no-interest account doesn't just "stay the same" — in *real* terms, it **shrinks**:
- KES 100,000 under the mattress, with 7% inflation, can buy only about **KES 93,000** worth of goods a year later. You didn't lose shillings — you lost *value*.
- This is why inflation is called a "silent tax": it quietly transfers value away from cash-holders, year after year, with no bill ever arriving.

The crucial distinction: **nominal vs real return.**
- **Nominal** = the headline number (your account "grew 4%").
- **Real** = nominal *minus inflation* (4% growth − 7% inflation = **−3% real** — you actually went backwards).

To genuinely grow your money, your return must **beat inflation.** Anything less and you're losing ground while feeling safe.

## Why the cost of living keeps rising

The cost of living in Kenya rises from a mix of: rising food prices (weather, supply), fuel costs (which feed into transport and everything moved by road), a **weaker shilling** making imports pricier (Series 3, Module 4), and general money-supply dynamics. Some years are mild; some spike. Kenya has seen sharp episodes — inflation hit roughly **19.7% in 2011**, surged in **2008**, and climbed again in **2022–23** before easing. Planning for a *normal* level of inflation (and the occasional spike) is just realism.

## How to protect your money from inflation

You can't stop inflation — but you can refuse to let it eat your savings. The rule: **keep your money in things that earn *more* than inflation.**

| Where | Return (2026) | Beats ~6.7% inflation? |
|---|---|---|
| Cash / no-interest account | ~0% | **No** — loses value |
| Low-interest bank savings | ~3–7% | Barely, often no |
| **Money market fund** | ~9% gross (~7.6% net) | **Yes** |
| **SACCO** (rebate/dividend) | ~10–20% | **Yes** |
| **Government / infrastructure bonds** | ~8–16% (IFBs tax-free) | **Yes** |

Other protections: holding **appreciating assets** (property, shares, a business) over the long term, which tend to rise with or above inflation; and avoiding holding large amounts of idle cash. Your **emergency fund** still needs to be accessible — but even it should sit in an MMF (which beats inflation) rather than a 0% account.

## Key takeaways

- **Inflation** is rising prices = falling purchasing power; KNBS measures it via the CPI (food ~32% of the basket).
- Kenya's inflation was ~**6.7%** in 2026, within the CBK's **2.5–7.5%** target band — but it has spiked to ~20% before.
- Cash and 0% accounts **lose real value** every year — inflation is a silent tax.
- Judge returns in **real** terms (nominal − inflation); your money must **beat inflation** to truly grow.
- Protect savings by keeping them in **inflation-beating** vehicles (MMF, SACCO, bonds) and appreciating assets — not idle cash.
