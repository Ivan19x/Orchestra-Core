# Government Securities: T-Bills, T-Bonds & DhowCSD

Here's one of the best-kept secrets of Kenyan personal finance: you can lend money directly to the government and earn a solid, low-risk return — the same instruments big banks and pension funds use. It used to require a bank and a lot of paperwork. Now, through the CBK's **DhowCSD** platform, an ordinary Kenyan can buy Treasury bills and bonds from their phone. This lesson explains how government securities work, what they pay, and how to actually buy them.

## What government securities are

When the government needs money, it borrows from the public by issuing **securities** — IOUs that pay you interest. Because they're backed by the government (which can tax and print), they're considered the **lowest-risk** investment available in the country. You're the lender; the government is the (very reliable) borrower.

There are two main types:

### Treasury bills (T-bills) — short-term
- Lent for **91, 182, or 364 days** (about 3, 6, or 12 months).
- Sold at a **discount**: you pay less than the face value and receive the full face value at maturity — the difference is your interest.
- Rates in 2026 have run roughly **8–9%** (e.g. 91-day ~7.6–8.8%, 364-day ~8.5%).
- Auctioned by the CBK — 91 and 182-day **weekly** (Wednesdays), 364-day **monthly**.
- Good for **short-term** money you want to park safely with a known return.

### Treasury bonds (T-bonds) — longer-term
- Lent for **2 to 30 years**.
- Pay you interest (a "coupon") **every six months**, then return your capital at maturity.
- Yields are competitive (often higher than T-bills, reflecting the longer term).
- Good for **longer-term** income — a steady half-yearly payment.

## The tax-free magic: infrastructure bonds

Among bonds, **infrastructure bonds (IFBs)** deserve special attention. The government issues them to fund specific infrastructure, and their standout feature is that their interest is **tax-free** (exempt from the withholding tax that applies to most interest). Recent IFBs have offered indicative yields around **13.5%–16%** — and because it's *tax-free*, the real take-home return is even more attractive relative to a taxed investment paying the same headline rate. This is why IFBs are heavily oversubscribed: a high, safe, tax-free return is rare. For a longer-horizon, low-risk investor, they're one of the most attractive options in Kenya.

## How to buy via DhowCSD

**DhowCSD** is the CBK's platform (app and web) that lets ordinary Kenyans buy government securities directly — no bank middleman:
1. **Register on DhowCSD** (CBK's portal/app) with your ID, KRA PIN, and a bank account.
2. **Watch the auction calendar** — the CBK announces what's on offer and when.
3. **Place a bid** for the security you want.
4. **Pay** — via bank transfer or **M-Pesa Paybill 200222**.
5. **Receive interest** automatically: T-bills pay at maturity; bonds pay coupons every six months into your account.

**The catch — the minimum.** Government securities require a **minimum of KES 100,000** (in multiples of KES 50,000). So this is a tool for once you've built up some capital — not your first KES 1,000. If you're not there yet, a **money market fund** (which itself invests heavily in these securities, from as little as ~KES 100) gives you indirect exposure with tiny minimums until you can buy directly.

## Where they fit in your money

- **Very low risk** — ideal for capital you want to protect while still earning well above a bank account.
- **T-bills** for short-term safe parking; **bonds** for longer-term income; **infrastructure bonds** for the best tax-free, longer-term return.
- **Laddering** — buying securities with staggered maturities — keeps some money always coming due, balancing access and return.
- A natural step up once your emergency fund (in an MMF) is set and you have capital to deploy safely.

## Key takeaways

- Government securities let you **lend to the government** for the **lowest-risk** return available.
- **T-bills** (91/182/364 days, ~8–9% in 2026) are short-term; **T-bonds** (2–30 years, half-yearly coupons) are longer-term.
- **Infrastructure bonds are tax-free** (~13.5–16% recently) — a rare high, safe, untaxed return.
- Buy directly via **DhowCSD** (pay by M-Pesa Paybill 200222), but note the **KES 100,000 minimum**.
- Below that minimum, an **MMF** gives indirect exposure with tiny minimums.
