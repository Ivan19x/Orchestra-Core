# Government Bonds & Treasury Bills

Every well-built investment portfolio needs a **safe anchor** — an asset that holds steady and pays reliably while riskier holdings (like shares) swing up and down. In Kenya, that anchor is **government securities**: Treasury bills and bonds. They offer a rare combination — genuinely low risk, returns that beat inflation, and, in the case of infrastructure bonds, *tax-free* income. This lesson looks at bills and bonds as **investments** and shows where they fit in your wealth-building plan.

> Series 4, Module 5 covers *what they are and how to buy them* (via DhowCSD). This lesson is about their *role in your portfolio*.

## The safe anchor of a portfolio

Investing well isn't about picking one winner — it's about **balancing** assets so the whole portfolio grows steadily without unbearable risk (Series 8, Module 8). Government securities play the "safe" role:
- **Shares** offer high growth but high volatility — they can fall for years.
- **Government bonds/bills** offer modest, *reliable* returns and barely move in value — the steady counterweight.

When shares wobble, your bonds keep paying — smoothing the ride and giving you stable income (or cash) you can rely on. A portfolio of only shares is a roller-coaster; adding bonds is the seatbelt.

## Bills vs bonds — as investments

- **Treasury bills** (91/182/364 days, ~**8–9%** in 2026) are for **shorter-term** safe money — a step up from an MMF for cash you won't need for a few months to a year, with a known return.
- **Treasury bonds** (2–30 years) pay you a **coupon every six months** — ideal for **steady long-term income**. A retiree, or anyone wanting predictable cash flow, can hold bonds and live partly off the half-yearly payments.

Because they're backed by the government, the risk of not being paid is very low — which is exactly why their return is lower than shares. You're buying *certainty*, and certainty is valuable.

## The standout: tax-free infrastructure bonds

For a long-term investor, **infrastructure bonds (IFBs)** are often the most attractive fixed-income option in Kenya. Two reasons:
1. **High yields** — recent IFBs have offered roughly **13.5%–16%**.
2. **Tax-free interest** — unlike ordinary bonds and bank/MMF interest (taxed at 15%), IFB interest is **exempt**.

That tax exemption is bigger than it looks. A tax-free 14% beats a *taxed* 14% meaningfully — because you keep all of it. For a low-risk asset to pay a high, *untaxed* return is genuinely rare, which is why IFBs are heavily oversubscribed. For long-horizon, safety-conscious money, they're hard to beat.

## Laddering: a simple strategy

A neat technique for bills and bonds is the **ladder**: instead of putting all your money in one security maturing on one date, you spread it across several with **staggered maturities** (e.g. some maturing in 1 year, some in 3, some in 5). Benefits:
- **Regular access** — something is always coming due, so you're never fully locked in.
- **Reinvestment at current rates** — as each matures, you reinvest at whatever rates then prevail, smoothing out rate changes.
- **Steady income** — coupons and maturities arrive on a rolling basis.

Laddering balances the trade-off between locking in good rates and keeping money accessible.

## Where bills and bonds fit in your plan

In the sensible order of building wealth:
1. **Emergency fund** in an MMF (accessible, beats inflation).
2. **Short-term safe money** → Treasury bills or MMF.
3. **Long-term safe anchor & income** → Treasury bonds, especially **tax-free IFBs.**
4. **Growth** → shares/property (the riskier, higher-return slice).

A balanced Kenyan portfolio might pair the steady income of bonds with the growth of shares and the strong returns of a SACCO — bonds being the part that lets you sleep at night.

Remember the practical floor: buying directly via DhowCSD needs a **KES 100,000 minimum**, so until you've built capital, an **MMF** (which itself holds these securities) gives you indirect exposure from as little as ~KES 100.

## Key takeaways

- Government securities are the **safe anchor** of a portfolio — steady returns that counterbalance volatile shares.
- **T-bills** (~8–9%) suit short-term safe money; **bonds** (half-yearly coupons) suit long-term steady income.
- **Tax-free infrastructure bonds** (~13.5–16%) are a standout — a high, *untaxed*, low-risk return.
- **Laddering** (staggered maturities) balances locking in rates with keeping money accessible.
- They're the "sleep at night" slice of a balanced plan; below the KES 100,000 DhowCSD minimum, an **MMF gives indirect exposure.**
