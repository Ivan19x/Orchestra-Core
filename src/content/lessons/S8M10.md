# Building a Long-Term Investment Plan

You've now met every tool — money market funds, SACCOs, government bonds, shares, property, pensions — and the principles that govern them. This final lesson of *Investing & Saving* pulls it all into a single, coherent **plan**: the right order to do things, how to split your money by goal and age, and how to actually stick with it over the decades that build real wealth. A plan turns scattered knowledge into results. Here is how to build yours.

## Why you need a plan (not just tools)

Knowing the tools isn't enough — used in the wrong order or without a strategy, even good tools fail. People invest in shares before they have an emergency fund, chase the highest return into something they don't understand, or start and stop with every market wobble. A **plan** prevents all that: it tells you *what to do with each shilling, in what order, for what goal* — so your money works as a system, not a series of random bets. The best plan is one you'll actually follow: simple, automatic, and matched to your life.

## The right order (the foundation sequence)

Do these in order — skipping ahead is how people get hurt:

1. **Budget so you have a surplus.** You can't invest what you don't free up (Series 1, Module 2).
2. **Clear expensive bad debt.** Paying off a mobile loan (~395%) or high-interest debt is the best "investment" you'll ever make — a guaranteed huge return (Series 1, Module 5).
3. **Build your emergency fund** — 3–6 months of essentials, in an MMF (Series 1, Module 7). This is your foundation; never skip it.
4. **Then invest for the future** — the money you can leave to grow, allocated by goal and time (below).

This sequence protects you: with no bad debt and a solid buffer, you can invest calmly and never be forced to sell at a bad time.

## Allocate by goal and time horizon

Once you're investing, match each pot of money to its **job and timeline**:

| Goal / horizon | Where it belongs |
|---|---|
| **Emergency fund** (anytime) | Money market fund — safe, accessible |
| **Short-term** (under ~2 years) | MMF, Treasury bills — low risk |
| **Medium-term** (2–7 years) | SACCO, bonds (incl. tax-free IFBs), conservative mix |
| **Long-term** (7+ years) | Shares/REITs, property, growth — time to ride out swings |
| **Retirement** | Pension (NSSF + employer + personal — tax-advantaged) |

The principle (Series 8, Module 8): **short-term money stays safe; long-term money can take more risk** to grow, because it has time to recover from dips. Never put money you'll need soon into volatile assets.

## Allocate by age too

Your mix shifts as you move through life:
- **Younger (20s–30s):** you have decades, so lean toward **growth** (more shares/property), accepting volatility for higher long-term returns.
- **Middle (40s–50s):** balance growth with more **safety** (bonds, SACCO) as goals near.
- **Near/in retirement:** shift toward **safe, income-producing** assets (bonds, MMF) to protect what you've built.

A simple Kenyan lifetime portfolio might blend: an **MMF** (emergency + flexible savings), a **SACCO** (strong returns + cheap credit), **bonds/IFBs** (safe income), **shares/REITs** (growth), and a **pension** (retirement) — weighted toward growth when young, toward safety as you age.

## Diversify, automate, and reinvest

Three habits make the plan work:
- **Diversify** across asset types and within them — the one free lunch (Series 8, Module 8). Never bet everything on one thing.
- **Automate** every contribution — a standing order into each pot on payday, so investing happens without willpower (Series 6, Module 2). This single habit, more than any clever pick, builds wealth.
- **Reinvest** all returns — dividends, interest, rebates — so compounding accelerates (Series 8, Module 2). Don't spend the growth; let it grow on itself.

## Stay the course (the hardest, most important part)

The greatest threat to your plan isn't the market — it's *you*, reacting emotionally. Wealth is built by **consistency over years**, and most people sabotage it by panic-selling in dips, chasing hype, or stopping when life gets busy. To stay the course:
- **Invest regularly, automatically**, through ups and downs — don't try to time the market.
- **Ignore the noise** — headlines, hot tips, FOMO, and fear (Series 3, Module 4; Series 6, Module 5). Your plan, not the news, decides your moves.
- **Review occasionally** (once or twice a year), rebalancing toward your targets — but don't tinker constantly.
- **Think in decades.** The plan's power comes from time; let compounding do the heavy lifting.
- **Increase contributions** as your income grows (bank the raise, Series 3, Module 7), and keep going.

The investor who simply keeps contributing to a sensible, diversified plan for 20–30 years almost always beats the clever one who jumps in and out. **Boring and consistent wins.**

## Your plan in one paragraph

Budget to create a surplus; clear bad debt; build a 3–6 month emergency fund in an MMF. Then automatically invest your long-term money across a diversified mix — MMF and bonds for safety, a SACCO for returns and cheap credit, shares or REITs for growth, and a pension for retirement — weighted toward growth while young and safety as you age. Reinvest everything, ignore the noise, increase contributions as you earn more, and stay the course for decades. That's it. That's how ordinary income becomes lasting wealth.

## Key takeaways

- A **plan** turns scattered tools into results: it tells you what to do with each shilling, in what order, for what goal.
- Follow the **order**: budget → clear bad debt → emergency fund → then invest.
- **Allocate by goal/time** (short-term safe, long-term growth) and **by age** (growth when young, safety when older).
- **Diversify, automate, and reinvest** — automation and consistency beat clever picks.
- **Stay the course** — ignore the noise, think in decades, increase contributions over time; boring and consistent wins.
