# Taxes for the Employed & Self-Employed

Knowing tax *rates* is one thing; actually *doing* your taxes is another — and it's the part that trips people up, leading to penalties and stress. Whether you earn a salary, run a hustle, or both, there are specific, practical steps you must take to stay on the right side of KRA. The good news: once you've done it once, it's routine. This lesson is the practical how-to of complying with tax as an individual and a small business.

> Series 4, Module 6 is the full tax reference. This lesson is about the *doing* — for employees and the self-employed specifically.

## If you're employed

The system mostly runs automatically — but not entirely:

**Your employer handles PAYE.** Each payday, your employer deducts **PAYE** (and NSSF, SHIF, AHL) from your salary and remits it to KRA by the 9th of the next month (Series 2, Module 1). You don't pay PAYE separately — it's already gone before you're paid.

**But you must still file an annual return.** This is the step people miss. Even though your employer deducted everything, **you are legally required to file your individual income tax return on iTax each year, by 30 June.** It's free. Here's how:
1. Log in to **iTax** with your KRA PIN.
2. Get your **P9 form** from your employer — it summarises your year's pay and PAYE.
3. Select the **income tax — resident individual** return; enter the figures from your P9 (often largely pre-filled).
4. Submit. If your employer deducted correctly, you usually owe nothing more — but you've **complied** and avoided the late-filing penalty.

**Filing isn't optional, and it isn't paying twice** — it's confirming what was already deducted. Missing it triggers an automatic penalty even though you owe no extra tax.

## If you're self-employed or hustling

Here you're responsible for your *own* tax — no employer does it for you. Your obligations depend on your income level:

**Get and use your KRA PIN**, and keep **simple records** of income and expenses from day one — even a notebook or a phone spreadsheet. Good records make everything below painless and show your true profit.

**Small business — Turnover Tax (TOT).** If your business turnover is between **KES 1 million and 25 million a year**, you generally pay **TOT at 3% of gross turnover** (sales, not profit), filed and paid monthly on iTax by the 20th. It's deliberately simple — no complex profit accounting needed at that scale.

**Below KES 1M turnover** — you're generally outside TOT, but you should still declare income on your annual return.

**Larger / VAT.** Once turnover exceeds **KES 5 million a year**, you must **register for VAT** (16%) and charge/remit it. Above the TOT ceiling (KES 25M), you move into full income-tax accounting.

**eTIMS — electronic invoicing.** KRA now requires businesses to issue **electronic tax invoices via eTIMS**. Even small businesses need to be on it (there are simplified options for the smallest). If you run any business, register for eTIMS and issue compliant invoices — increasingly, expenses without an eTIMS invoice aren't recognised.

## The employed-plus-hustle case (very common)

Many people have a salary *and* a side hustle. You combine both:
- Your **employer handles PAYE** on the salary.
- You **declare the side-hustle income** too — on your annual return, and via TOT/VAT/eTIMS if the business grows past the thresholds.
- File your **annual return** covering both.

Don't assume the hustle is "invisible" to KRA — declare it; the penalties for hiding income are worse than the modest tax on it.

## Staying compliant without stress

- **Get your KRA PIN early** and keep your iTax login safe.
- **Mark the deadlines:** annual return **30 June**; TOT/VAT **20th**; PAYE (employer) 9th.
- **Keep records** all year so filing season isn't a scramble.
- **File even a nil return** if you had no income — it avoids the penalty.
- **When unsure, ask KRA or a tax pro** — a small fee for help beats a big penalty.

Compliance is mostly about *routine*: PIN, records, deadlines, file. Do those and tax stops being scary.

## Key takeaways

- **Employed:** your employer remits PAYE, but **you must still file your annual return on iTax by 30 June** (using your P9) — it's free and not paying twice.
- **Self-employed:** you handle your own tax — get a PIN, **keep records**, and pay **TOT (3%)** if turnover is KES 1–25M.
- **Register for VAT** above KES 5M turnover, and get on **eTIMS** for electronic invoices if you run any business.
- **Employed + hustle:** declare both and file one annual return — don't assume side income is invisible.
- Compliance is **routine**: PIN, records, deadlines, file (even a nil return).
