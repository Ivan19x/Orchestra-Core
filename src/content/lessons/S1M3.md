# Saving: How to Keep the Money You Make

Earning money and *keeping* money are two completely different skills. Plenty of people who earn well are still broke at month-end, because every shilling that comes in finds a way out. Saving is the bridge between earning and building wealth — and it's less about discipline than most people think, and more about setting things up so saving happens almost on its own. This lesson shows why saving is hard, the one trick that fixes it, and exactly where to keep your money in Kenya so it grows instead of leaking.

## Why saving is so hard

It isn't (mostly) that you're undisciplined. Saving is hard because:
- **You save what's left** — and after life happens, nothing is left.
- **Spending is easy and instant**, while saving's reward is far away and fuzzy (the present-bias trap from the psychology series).
- **Money in reach gets spent.** Cash in your M-Pesa wallet or current account is too easy to dip into.
- **Pressure** — black tax, social spending, and "offers" all pull at your balance.

If you've struggled to save, you're normal. The fix isn't trying harder — it's changing the *order*.

## The one rule: pay yourself first

Flip the equation. Instead of `income − expenses = savings`, do **`income − savings = expenses`**. The moment money arrives, move a fixed slice to savings *first*, then live on the rest. This single change beats years of willpower because you can't spend money that's already gone.

How much? A common target is **20% of income**, but **any** amount started now beats a bigger amount "someday." Even 10% — or, on an irregular income, a slice of *whatever* arrives — builds the habit and the balance. **Automate it**: a standing order or auto-transfer on payday makes saving the default, not a decision.

## Where to actually keep your savings in Kenya

Not all savings homes are equal. Match the place to the job:

| Where | Typical return (2026) | Access | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Current/wallet** | ~0% | Instant | *Nothing* — loses to inflation |
| **M-Shwari / mobile** | Low | Instant (Lock Savings to resist) | Tiny amounts, getting started |
| **Money market fund (MMF)** | ~9% gross (~7.6% after 15% tax) | 1–3 days | Emergency fund & general savings |
| **SACCO deposits** | ~10–13% interest/rebate | Locked-in, backs cheap loans | Disciplined long-term saving |
| **Bank fixed deposit** | Varies, guaranteed | Locked for a term | A lump sum you won't touch |

The big lesson: **don't leave savings in a zero-interest wallet**, where inflation (~6.7% in 2026) quietly shrinks them. A decent **MMF or SACCO beats inflation**, so your money holds its value and grows. For most people, an **MMF makes an excellent everyday savings home** (good return, quick access), while a **SACCO** rewards disciplined long-term saving with strong rates *and* access to cheap loans later.

## The maths of small, consistent saving

People underestimate how much "small but steady" adds up, because money grows on itself. Save **KES 2,000 a month** into a ~9% fund and leave it:
- In **5 years** you've put in KES 120,000 — it's worth about **KES 151,000**.
- In **10 years** you've put in KES 240,000 — it's worth about **KES 387,000**.

The growth accelerates the longer you leave it (the full compounding story is in Series 8, Module 2). The point: you don't need a big income to build real money — you need a small amount, every month, started now, somewhere that earns.

## Betting: saving in reverse

A hard truth worth naming. Betting is the exact opposite of saving — it takes your money and, on average, gives back less, fast. It's a particularly heavy drain among young Kenyan men, and it quietly destroys what saving builds: instead of small amounts growing into wealth, small amounts vanish into losses. If you bet, track what you've *actually* spent over a month — the number is usually shocking, and redirected into an MMF it would compound into something real. Treat money you'd bet as money you'd save.

## Key takeaways

- Saving is hard because you **save what's left** — so flip it: **pay yourself first**, then live on the rest.
- **Automate** a fixed slice on payday; any amount now beats more "someday."
- **Don't leave savings in a 0% wallet** — use an **MMF (~9%)** or **SACCO (~10–13%)** that beats inflation.
- Small, steady saving compounds: **KES 2,000/month → ~KES 387,000 in 10 years.**
- **Betting is saving in reverse** — redirect it into savings.
