# Money Market Funds Explained

If there's one tool every saving Kenyan should understand, it's the **money market fund (MMF)**. It's the practical sweet spot between a lifeless bank account and risky investing: low risk, quick access, and a return that comfortably beats inflation — all from as little as a few hundred shillings. It's where your emergency fund and general savings should probably live. This lesson explains exactly how MMFs work, what they really pay, and how to choose one well.

## What an MMF actually is

A money market fund is a **collective investment** — a pool where many people's money is gathered and managed by a licensed fund manager (regulated by the **CMA**). The manager invests the pool in safe, short-term instruments: Treasury bills, fixed deposits, and high-quality short-term debt. The interest earned, minus a small management fee, is shared among investors — credited to you **daily**.

Think of it as a savings vehicle that earns near-Treasury returns, that you can join with little money and exit within days. It is *low* risk (not zero — but losses are very rare for well-run shilling MMFs), which is exactly why it suits savings rather than long-term wealth-growth.

## What MMFs pay in Kenya (2026)

Honest numbers, current as of 2026:
- **Average yield:** about **9% gross** across the industry's funds.
- **Range:** roughly **5% to 13%** depending on the fund.
- **After tax:** returns are quoted *gross*. A **15% withholding tax** applies, so a 9% gross fund nets about **7.6%** to you.

Crucially, that ~7.6% net is **above inflation** (~6.7% in 2026), so your money holds its real value and grows — unlike a 0% current account, which loses to inflation every year. (Note: MMF yields drift with interest rates — as the CBK cut rates through 2025–26, yields eased down, so always check the current figure.)

## The features that make MMFs great for saving

- **Low minimums.** Many funds start from around **KES 100–1,000**, and let you add small amounts anytime. No need for a big lump sum.
- **Quick access.** Withdrawals typically reach you in **1–3 working days** — slow enough to discourage impulse spending, fast enough for a real emergency.
- **Daily interest, compounding.** Interest is calculated daily and reinvested, so your money compounds (Series 8, Module 2) without you doing anything.
- **No lock-in.** Unlike a fixed deposit or SACCO share, you're not tied in.
- **Hands-off.** A professional manages it; you just contribute and let it run.

This combination — beats inflation, starts tiny, accessible in days — is why an MMF is the ideal home for an **emergency fund** and everyday savings.

## How to compare funds (it's not just the headline rate)

The fund with the biggest advertised yield is **not** automatically the best. Compare on:
- **Net, consistent yield** — look at the return *after* fees and over time, not a single flashy week. Steady beats spiky.
- **The fund manager's credibility** — is it a reputable, **CMA-licensed** manager with a track record and audited accounts? A slightly lower yield from a solid manager beats a high yield from a shaky one.
- **Fees** — the management fee is already reflected in the yield, but understand it.
- **Minimums and top-up rules** — does it fit how you'll actually save?
- **Access and ease** — how you deposit/withdraw (M-Pesa integration, app), and how fast.
- **Safety over yield.** This is your *safe* money. Chasing the top rate into an obscure or unregulated fund defeats the purpose — the whole point of an MMF is low risk.

## How to start

1. Pick a **CMA-licensed** fund manager (the big, established names, or check the CMA list).
2. Open the account (often online/app, with ID and KRA PIN).
3. Fund it — many accept **M-Pesa** from small amounts.
4. **Automate a monthly top-up** so it compounds.
5. Keep your **emergency fund** here, and use it as the parking spot for savings before you invest elsewhere.

## Key takeaways

- An MMF is a **low-risk, CMA-regulated pool** investing in safe short-term instruments, paying interest **daily**.
- It pays about **9% gross / ~7.6% after the 15% tax** in 2026 — **above inflation**, unlike a 0% account.
- Great for saving: **low minimums (~KES 100), access in 1–3 days, no lock-in, compounding.**
- **Highest yield ≠ best** — weigh net consistent yield *and* the manager's credibility; safety first.
- Ideal home for your **emergency fund** and savings-before-investing.
