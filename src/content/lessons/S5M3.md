# Avoiding Debt Traps Young

Two things destroy more young Kenyans' finances than anything else, and both are designed to feel harmless: **mobile loans** and **betting**. They're on the same phone you're reading this on, available in seconds, marketed relentlessly, and engineered to keep you coming back. Get caught young — listed on the CRB, stuck in a loan cycle, chasing losses — and you start adult life in a hole. Avoid them, and you've dodged the biggest wealth-killers of your generation. This lesson shows exactly how the traps work and how to stay free.

## Trap 1: the mobile loan cycle

App loans and overdrafts (Fuliza and the dozens of loan apps) feel like a friend in a tight spot. The danger is the **cycle**: you borrow to cover a gap, repayment eats into next month, so you borrow again — and round it goes.

The killer is the *real* cost, which is hidden by tiny-looking daily fees. **Fuliza's** small daily charge annualises to roughly **395% a year** — astronomically expensive. Many loan apps are similar or worse once you annualise their "small" fees. Borrow KES 1,000 and the fee feels like nothing; live in that borrowing and it bleeds you dry.

How the trap catches you young:
- **Instant and easy** — no real friction, available at 2am when judgment is low.
- **Tiny fees disguise huge rates** — you never do the annual maths.
- **Auto-deduction** — repayments vanish from your M-Pesa before you feel them, so you lose track.

## Trap 2: betting

Betting is marketed as smart, fun, and a path to quick money. The maths says the opposite: on average, bettors **lose** — that's how the industry profits. It hits **young men especially hard** in Kenya, and it's psychologically engineered to hook you: near-misses, the thrill of the win, and "chasing losses" (betting more to win back what you lost, which usually digs the hole deeper).

Betting isn't a money strategy — it's the reverse of saving. Every shilling bet is, on average, a shilling shrinking. The same KES 500 a week put into a money market fund would compound into real money over a few years; bet, it mostly vanishes.

## The CRB: getting listed young follows you

When you default on a regulated loan, you can be reported to a **Credit Reference Bureau (CRB)**. A negative listing can:
- **Block future credit** — a SACCO loan, a phone contract, sometimes even a job check — for years.
- **Follow you** into the exact season of life (first job, first business) when you'll need credit most.

Getting CRB-listed at 20 over a KES 2,000 app loan is a genuinely costly mistake. (More on the CRB in Series 2, Module 8.)

## How to stay out of the traps

- **Build a small buffer first.** Even a tiny emergency fund (Series 1) removes the "I had no choice" reason to borrow. Most app loans are taken to cover gaps a small buffer would have covered.
- **Do the annual maths before you tap.** Ask: "what does this cost *per year*?" A ~395% number is hard to unsee. If you wouldn't accept the annual rate, don't accept the daily one.
- **Delete the temptation.** Uninstall loan and betting apps. You can't impulse-tap what isn't there.
- **Never bet money you can't lose — and recognise you'll likely lose it.** If you bet at all, treat it strictly as a capped entertainment cost, never an income plan, and never chase losses.
- **If you're already in a loan cycle:** stop new borrowing, list every debt with its real rate, clear the most expensive first, and replace app loans with a far cheaper source (a SACCO, or the Hustler Fund at ~8%/yr) where possible.
- **Redirect the habit.** The money you'd bet or the fees you'd pay, sent to an MMF instead, builds the opposite of a trap.

## Key takeaways

- The two big young-person traps are **mobile loans** and **betting** — both instant, addictive, and far costlier than they look.
- **Fuliza/app loans annualise to ~395%+** — tiny daily fees hide brutal rates.
- **Betting loses on average** and is engineered to hook you — it's saving in reverse.
- A **CRB default at a young age** blocks credit for years, right when you'll need it.
- Stay free: build a small **buffer**, do the **annual maths**, **delete the apps**, and redirect the money into savings.
