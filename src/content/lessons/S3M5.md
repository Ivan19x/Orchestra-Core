# How Banks & Lenders Make Money

Banks are among the most profitable businesses in Kenya, year after year. Understanding *how* they make that money isn't academic — it's the key to flipping the relationship in your favour. Once you see exactly where a bank earns from you, you can position yourself to pay it the least and earn from it the most. The financially smart aren't anti-bank; they just refuse to be the customer the bank profits most from. This lesson shows you how the machine works.

## The core engine: the interest spread

A bank's main business is beautifully simple: **take in money cheaply, lend it out expensively, keep the difference.**

- It pays *you*, the depositor, a low rate on your savings — averaging about **7%** in Kenya (2026), and as little as **3%** at some banks.
- It lends that same money to borrowers at a much higher rate — averaging about **14.78%**, up to **18%+** at some banks.

That gap — roughly **7.8 percentage points** — is the **interest spread** (or net interest margin), and it's where banks make most of their money. Your deposit, paying you 7%, is lent to someone else at 15%; the bank pockets the difference. Multiply that across billions of shillings and you see why banks are so profitable.

**The lesson for you:** you're on the *wrong* side of the spread when you keep large savings in a low-interest bank account (earning ~3–7% or less) while borrowing at high rates. The smart move is to be a *lender* yourself — putting savings where they earn more (MMF ~9%, SACCO 10–20%) and borrowing, when you must, from the cheapest source.

## The second engine: fees

Beyond the spread, banks earn a fortune in **fees** — and these are the ones you can most easily avoid:
- Monthly/ledger maintenance fees
- Withdrawal, transfer and ATM charges
- Bank-to-M-Pesa fees
- SMS/alert charges
- Below-minimum-balance and dormancy penalties
- Loan facility/processing fees

Individually small, collectively large — and largely *optional* if you choose accounts and habits wisely (Series 2, Module 2). Every fee you avoid is pure money kept.

## Why mobile loans are *so* profitable

Mobile lenders take the bank model and supercharge it. Their secret is the **annualised rate hidden behind a tiny fee.** A "one-off 7.5%" or a "small daily charge" feels trivial, but on short loans repaid and re-borrowed constantly, the effective annual return to the lender is enormous — Fuliza-style products annualise to roughly **395%**. Add huge volume (millions of small loans) and near-instant, automated lending, and you have one of the most profitable lending models ever built — profitable precisely *because* borrowers don't compute the annual cost. You're the product. The defence is simple: do the annual maths, and don't be the volume they profit from.

## How to be the customer who wins

Flip the relationship using everything above:
- **Be a lender, not just a depositor.** Move idle savings out of low-interest accounts into MMFs (~9%), SACCOs (10–20%), or bonds — earn the *higher* side of the spread.
- **Borrow only from the cheapest source** (SACCO, structured bank loan), and avoid mobile loans except true emergencies.
- **Kill the fees.** Choose low/zero-fee accounts, pay by Buy Goods (free), avoid needless withdrawals, audit charges yearly.
- **Use the bank as a tool, not a home for wealth.** A bank account is for spending and transacting; your *wealth* should live where it earns more.
- **Read every loan's APR.** Refuse to be the borrower whose ignorance is someone's profit.

You don't beat the bank by avoiding it — you beat it by understanding its model and positioning yourself on the profitable side of every transaction.

## Key takeaways

- Banks' main profit is the **interest spread** — paying depositors ~7% and lending at ~14.78% (a ~7.8pt gap).
- They also earn heavily from **fees**, most of which you can avoid with smart account choices.
- **Mobile loans are ultra-profitable** because tiny fees hide ~395% annualised rates on huge volume.
- Win by being a **lender** (savings in MMF/SACCO/bonds), borrowing from the **cheapest** source, and **killing fees.**
- Use the bank as a **transaction tool**, not the home for your wealth.
