# Money Skills Before You Leave Home

Moving out is one of life's great milestones — and one of its great financial shocks. Suddenly *everything* costs money: the rent, the food, the tokens, the gas, the little things home always quietly provided. Many young people leap before they're ready and spend their first independent months stressed, broke, and borrowing. This final lesson of *Teenager to Adult* is your pre-flight checklist: the money skills and the cushion to have in place *before* you go, so independence is exciting rather than terrifying.

## What changes when you leave home

At home, many costs are invisible because someone else covers them — rent, electricity, water, food, internet, "small" things. The moment you move out, **all of it becomes yours**, all at once. The single biggest mistake young movers make is underestimating the *total* monthly cost of living alone — and the *upfront* cost of setting up (Series 2, Module 3). Going in with eyes open is half the battle.

## The skills checklist

Before you move out, make sure you can confidently do each of these (and where you can't yet, this is your study list):

- **Budget** — build and stick to a monthly budget on your real income (Series 1, Module 2). This is the master skill; nothing else works without it.
- **Tell needs from wants** — so your limited money covers essentials first (Series 1, Module 4).
- **Save and keep an emergency fund** — pay yourself first, even living alone (Series 1, Modules 3 & 7).
- **Manage bills** — understand tokens, gas, water, internet, and how to control them (Series 2, Module 4).
- **Cook affordably** — home cooking is one of the biggest savings vs eating out (Series 7, Module 3).
- **Use M-Pesa and accounts well** — minimise fees, keep savings separate (Series 4, Module 2).
- **Avoid the debt traps** — no reaching for mobile loans or betting when money's tight (Series 5, Module 3).
- **Do basic taxes** — have a KRA PIN and know your obligations if working (Series 5, Module 4).

If several of those feel shaky, that's not a reason to panic — it's a reason to learn them now, while the stakes are low and home is still a safety net.

## The financial cushion you need first

Skills aren't enough — you need money in place before you go:
1. **The full move-in cost saved** — deposit + first month's rent + moving + basic furnishing. This is often **several months' rent** (Series 2, Module 3). Sink-fund it *before* you commit, so you arrive with a buffer, not a hole.
2. **An emergency fund** — even a small one (a month of costs to start). Living alone means *you* handle every surprise; without a buffer, the first shock sends you to a loan app.
3. **A stable enough income** — confidence that you can cover the monthly cost of living alone, with margin. If your income barely covers the rent, you're not ready yet — and that's fine.

## A realistic first-month budget

Before signing anything, write out your **actual** monthly cost of living alone, honestly:

| Item | Plan for |
|---|---|
| Rent | Your biggest cost — keep it ≤ ~30% of income |
| Food | Cooking at home (the controllable big one) |
| Transport | Fare/fuel to work and around |
| Utilities | Electricity tokens, water, gas, internet |
| Airtime/data | A right-sized bundle |
| Savings | Pay yourself first — yes, even now |
| Buffer | A little slack for the unexpected |

Total it, compare to your **take-home income**, and check it leaves room to save and breathe. If it doesn't balance, you either need a cheaper setup (sharing — Series 7, Module 2), more income, or a bit more time. **Better to delay and move out solid than to leap and struggle.**

## A smart sequence

- **Learn the skills** while still at home (low stakes).
- **Save the full move-in cost + a starter emergency fund** before committing.
- **Run the real monthly budget** and confirm it balances with room to spare.
- **Consider sharing first** to build your base, then live alone once solid (Series 7, Module 2).
- **Move out on a plan, not an impulse** — and enjoy the independence you've actually prepared for.

## Key takeaways

- Leaving home makes **every cost yours at once** — and people badly underestimate the total monthly and upfront cost.
- Have the core **skills** first: budgeting, needs vs wants, saving, managing bills, cooking, using accounts well, avoiding debt traps.
- Have the **cushion** first: the full move-in cost saved, a starter emergency fund, and a stable-enough income.
- **Write a realistic first-month budget** and confirm it balances with room to save.
- **Move out prepared, not impulsively** — consider sharing first; delaying to go solid beats leaping to struggle.
