# Multiple Income Streams & Side Hustles

Relying on a single income is one of the most common — and most dangerous — money positions to be in. If that one salary stops (a layoff, a sick month, a business that slows), everything stops with it. The financially secure rarely depend on one source. In Kenya, this isn't elite advice; it's how most people already live — the *hustle* is a national instinct. This lesson turns that instinct into a strategy.

## Why one income is risky

Think of income like a stool. A one-legged stool falls the moment that leg slips. Add legs and it stands even if one wobbles. A single salary means:
- **No cushion** if you lose the job or the business dips.
- **No leverage** to negotiate, because you can't walk away.
- **Slow progress** — one income caps how fast you can save and invest.

A second or third stream changes your whole position: it funds your savings faster, protects you from shocks, and can eventually grow into something bigger than the first.

## The three kinds of extra income

Not all side income is equal. Broadly:
1. **Active hustles** — you trade time/skill for money (tutoring, freelance design, weekend catering, fixing phones, a small shop run after hours). Fast to start, but stops when you stop.
2. **Business income** — you build something that runs partly without you (a stall with an employee, online reselling, a service with repeat clients). More work upfront, more freedom later.
3. **Passive/asset income** — money your *money* earns: SACCO dividends, money market fund interest, rent, government-bond interest. The goal you build toward (see Series 8).

A smart path often runs in that order: start with an active hustle, grow it into a small business, and channel the profits into assets that eventually pay you without your time.

## Realistic Kenyan side hustles

The best side hustle uses a skill or asset you already have. Common, real options:
- **Skills:** tutoring, graphic design, writing, photography, web/social-media management, tailoring, hairdressing, phone/laptop repair.
- **Trade/reselling:** *mitumba* (second-hand clothes), thrift flipping, cosmetics, electronics accessories, buying in bulk and selling in units.
- **Food:** snacks, baked goods, weekend catering, a lunch service for offices.
- **Online/digital:** virtual assistance, transcription, content creation, online tutoring, selling digital products.
- **Asset-based:** renting out a spare room, a *boda* you own, equipment hire.

Pick something with **real demand**, low start-up cost, and that fits around your main job. Start small, test whether people actually pay, then grow what works.

## Treat the hustle like a business, not pocket money

The difference between a hustle that grows and one that fizzles is basic discipline:
- **Separate its money** from your personal money — a different M-Pesa till or account — so you can see if it's actually profitable.
- **Know your real profit.** Revenue minus *all* costs (stock, transport, data, M-Pesa fees, your time). Many "busy" hustles make little once you count everything.
- **Reinvest early.** In the first stretch, plough profits back in to grow, rather than spending them.
- **Don't let it cost your main income.** Protect the salary that funds your life until the hustle can stand on its own.

## Don't forget the taxman

Side income is still income, and KRA expects it on your annual return. A few essentials:
- Get and use your **KRA PIN**; declare side earnings when you file on **iTax**.
- Small businesses may fall under **Turnover Tax (TOT)** — a simple tax on turnover for businesses between roughly KES 1 million and 25 million a year.
- Once a business crosses the **VAT threshold (KES 5 million turnover)**, VAT obligations kick in, and **eTIMS** electronic invoicing applies to businesses generally.

You don't need to be a tax expert on day one, but build the habit of keeping simple records from the start — it makes compliance painless and shows your true profit. (Series 2, Module 7 and Series 4, Module 6 go deeper.)

## Key takeaways

- One income is fragile; **extra streams** add stability, leverage and speed.
- Income comes in three forms — **active hustle → business → passive/asset** — and a smart path moves along that line.
- Choose hustles with **real demand and low start-up cost** that fit around your main job.
- Run the hustle like a business: **separate its money, know true profit, reinvest early.**
- Side income is **taxable** — keep records, use your KRA PIN, and learn TOT/VAT thresholds.
