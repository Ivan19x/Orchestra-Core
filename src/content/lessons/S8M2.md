# The Power of Compound Interest

Albert Einstein supposedly called compound interest "the eighth wonder of the world — he who understands it, earns it; he who doesn't, pays it." Whether or not he said it, it's true. Compound interest is the quiet engine behind all long-term wealth — and the same force, working in reverse, is what makes Fuliza and mobile loans so dangerous. Understand this one idea deeply and the whole logic of saving early finally clicks. This lesson makes it concrete with Kenyan shillings.

## Simple vs compound: interest on interest

- **Simple interest** pays you only on your original money.
- **Compound interest** pays you on your original money *and* on all the interest it has already earned. Your interest starts earning interest.

A quick illustration. Put KES 10,000 in a fund earning 10% a year:
- Year 1: you earn 10% of 10,000 = KES 1,000 → balance KES 11,000.
- Year 2: you earn 10% of **11,000** (not 10,000) = KES 1,100 → balance KES 12,100.
- Year 3: 10% of 12,100 = KES 1,210 → balance KES 13,310.

Each year the gain is bigger than the last, because you're earning on a bigger base. Early on it feels slow; over time it becomes a snowball.

## The real Kenyan example: KES 5,000 a month

Here's the number that should make you start today. Save **KES 5,000 every month** into a money market fund averaging about **9% a year** (a realistic Kenyan MMF, before the 15% withholding tax). Watch what happens:

| You save for | You put in | It grows to (~9%) |
|---|---|---|
| 5 years | KES 300,000 | **≈ KES 377,000** |
| 10 years | KES 600,000 | **≈ KES 968,000** |
| 20 years | KES 1,200,000 | **≈ KES 3,340,000** |

Look at the 20-year line: you contributed KES 1.2 million, but ended with about **KES 3.3 million** — over **two million shillings** of it is pure growth you didn't deposit. That's compounding. (Tax trims it slightly; the shape is what matters.)

Notice too how the growth *accelerates*: the jump from year 10 to year 20 is far bigger than year 0 to year 10. The longer it runs, the more the snowball does the work instead of you.

## The Rule of 72: a shortcut in your head

Want to know how fast money doubles? Divide **72 by the annual return**:
- At 9%, money doubles in about `72 ÷ 9 =` **8 years**.
- At 12%, about **6 years**.
- At 6%, about **12 years**.

It's the flip side that's chilling: a Fuliza-style cost of roughly 1% *per day* doubles what you owe in about **72 days**. The same maths that builds wealth slowly destroys it fast when you're on the wrong side of it.

## The cost of waiting

Because time is the most powerful ingredient, **delay is expensive** — far more than people expect. Two friends each save KES 5,000/month into the same 9% fund:
- **Wanjiru** starts at 25 and saves for 30 years.
- **Otieno** waits just 10 years, starts at 35, and saves for 20 years.

Otieno only "lost" 10 years of contributions (KES 600,000) — but because those early shillings would have had the longest time to compound, he ends up with **dramatically less** than Wanjiru, not just a little less. The lesson is brutal and freeing: **the best time to start was years ago; the second-best time is now.** Waiting "until I earn more" usually costs more than the bigger contributions ever make up.

## How to put compounding to work

- **Start now, even small.** Time matters more than amount. KES 1,000 a month started today beats KES 5,000 started in five years.
- **Be consistent.** Automatic monthly contributions are how the snowball gets fed.
- **Reinvest the returns.** Don't withdraw the interest/dividends — let them compound. (SACCO members who reinvest dividends feel this powerfully.)
- **Give it time and leave it alone.** Compounding rewards patience; pulling money out early kills the snowball.
- **Mind the reverse.** The same force makes high-interest debt explode — clear it fast.

## Key takeaways

- **Compound interest pays you on your interest too** — gains accelerate over time.
- KES 5,000/month at ~9% becomes **~KES 3.3M in 20 years** from KES 1.2M of contributions — the rest is growth.
- **Rule of 72:** years to double ≈ 72 ÷ return (≈ 8 years at 9%).
- **Waiting is costly** — early shillings compound the longest; start now, even small.
- The same force works in reverse on debt — which is why high-interest loans are so dangerous.
