# Avoiding Scams, Fraud & Get-Rich-Quick Schemes

Every year, Kenyans lose enormous sums to scams — Ponzi schemes promising "double your money," fake forex and crypto "investments," fraudulent SACCOs, and M-Pesa cons. The victims aren't foolish; the schemes are sophisticated, persuasive, and prey on hope and urgency. But almost every scam shares the same handful of warning signs — and once you can spot them, you become very hard to fool. This final lesson of *Adult Life Money* is your defence: how to recognise scams, verify what's real, and protect your money and your family.

## The universal red flag: returns too good to be true

If you remember one thing, remember this: **guaranteed high returns with little or no risk are always a lie.** It's the single most reliable scam detector. Real investing follows the risk–return rule (Series 8, Module 8) — higher returns *always* mean higher risk, and nothing legitimate guarantees big, fast, risk-free profits. So when someone promises "30% a month," "double your money in 90 days," or "guaranteed daily profits," your answer is automatic: **it's a scam.** No exceptions. The CMA itself warns: schemes luring people with "unusually high returns" leave victims with no protection when the money vanishes.

## The classic Kenyan scams

### Ponzi and pyramid schemes
These pay early "investors" with money from new ones — not from any real business. They collapse when recruitment slows, and most people lose everything. Tell-tale signs:
- **Pressure to recruit** friends and family ("the more you bring, the more you earn") — the hallmark of a pyramid.
- **Returns that depend on new members joining**, not a real product or service.
- **Upfront fees** to "join" or to "unlock your earnings."
Kenya has seen waves of these — fake investment clubs, "forex trading" schemes, dubious crypto platforms.

### Fake forex, crypto & online "trading"
Unlicensed platforms and "account managers" promise to trade your money for huge returns. The CMA has repeatedly warned against **unlicensed forex, crypto, and MMF schemes.** Many are outright theft — your money never gets traded; it's just gone.

### Fake SACCOs and investment companies
Fraudsters mimic legitimate SACCOs or fund managers. **Defence:** verify licensing (below) — a real SACCO is on SASRA's list; a real fund manager on the CMA register.

### "You've won" / advance-fee scams
The classic: a message says you've won a prize or money, but you must **"send a small fee" to receive it.** You never receive anything. **Rule: you never pay to receive money you've won.** If you have to send money to get money, it's a scam.

### M-Pesa and mobile-money fraud
- **The "wrong send" trick** — someone claims they sent you money by mistake and pressures you to send it back. **Check your real balance first** — the SMS may be fake (Series 4, Module 2).
- **SIM-swap fraud** — criminals hijack your number to reach your M-Pesa. Use a strong SIM PIN; act fast if your line goes dead.
- **PIN/OTP phishing** — anyone calling to ask for your M-Pesa PIN or an OTP is a fraudster. Safaricom never does this. Hang up.
- **Fake agents and fake job offers** demanding payment.

## How to verify before you send a shilling

Before putting money into *any* investment or scheme:
1. **Check it's licensed.** Confirm the company/fund/broker is on the regulator's official register — **CMA** (cma.or.ke) for investments, funds, forex; **SASRA** for SACCOs; **CBK** for banks and digital lenders; **IRA** for insurance. The CMA states plainly: **"No licence equals no accountability"** — if it's unlicensed, they can't help you recover a cent.
2. **Look for the other red flags** — no verifiable physical address, no audited accounts, no clear registration, pressure to act *now*, difficulty withdrawing your money, or returns that only flow if you recruit.
3. **Slow down.** Scams rely on urgency ("offer ends today," "limited slots"). Anything legitimate survives you taking a few days to verify and think.
4. **Ask "how does it actually make money?"** If the answer is vague, or it's really just new members' money, walk away.

## If you spot or fall for a scam

- **Don't send more** (scammers often demand "one more fee" to "release" your money — never coming).
- **Report it** — to the police/DCI, the relevant regulator (CMA, CBK), and for mobile-money fraud, your provider. Reporting helps protect others even if recovery is hard.
- **Warn your circle** — these schemes spread through trusted networks; a warning can save a friend.
- **Reverse an erroneous M-Pesa** by forwarding the message to **456**.

## Protecting yourself and your family

- **Internalise the golden rule:** guaranteed high, fast, risk-free returns = scam. Always.
- **Verify licensing** before any investment, every time.
- **Guard your PINs and OTPs** — share with no one, ever.
- **Distrust urgency and "recruit your friends"** pitches.
- **Talk to family** — especially relatives who may be targeted; share these red flags.
- **Invest only through legitimate, licensed channels** (the ones taught across this curriculum: SACCOs, MMFs, the NSE, government securities).

## Key takeaways

- **Guaranteed high, fast, risk-free returns are ALWAYS a scam** — the single most reliable red flag.
- Know the classics: **Ponzi/pyramid schemes** (pay to join, recruit to earn), **fake forex/crypto**, **fake SACCOs**, **"pay a fee to receive your prize,"** and **M-Pesa fraud** (wrong-send, SIM-swap, PIN phishing).
- **Verify licensing** before sending money — CMA (investments), SASRA (SACCOs), CBK (banks/lenders), IRA (insurance). *No licence = no protection.*
- **Slow down** — scams rely on urgency; legitimate things survive verification.
- **Guard PINs/OTPs**, report scams, and warn your family.
