# Understanding Loans & Credit

Sooner or later, adult life involves borrowing — for a deposit, a business, an emergency, or just a tight month. The difference between debt that helps and debt that traps usually comes down to *which* loan you pick and whether you actually understood its terms. Most people borrow from whatever's easiest (the app on their phone) and never read the real cost. This lesson maps the loan landscape, teaches you to read any loan's true price, and shows how to get out if you're already stuck.

> Series 1, Module 5 covers the *good vs bad debt* principle. This lesson is the practical product guide.

## The loan landscape, cheapest to costliest

Where you borrow matters enormously. Roughly from cheapest to most expensive (2026):

| Source | Typical cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **SACCO loan** | ~1%/month reducing (~low-teens %/yr) | Cheapest serious credit; needs deposits + guarantors |
| **Hustler Fund** | ~8%/year | Cheapest for small government micro-loans |
| **Bank loan** | ~10.8%–18.6%/year (avg ~14.78%) | For larger, structured borrowing |
| **Mortgage (KMRC-backed)** | ~9%/year | Long-term home buying |
| **M-Shwari / KCB M-Pesa** | one-off facility fee (e.g. ~7.5%) | Convenient, short-term |
| **Mobile loan apps / Fuliza** | tiny daily fee → **~395%/yr** | Emergencies only — brutally expensive |

The single most important habit: **for any real borrowing, go to a SACCO or bank first, not the app.** The convenience of mobile loans is paid for many times over in their annualised cost.

## How to read a loan's true terms

Lenders advertise the *small* number and hide the *real* one. Before you sign or tap, find:
- **The APR (annual percentage rate)** — the true yearly cost. Convert any "per day" or "per month" fee to a year (×365 or ×12). A 1%/day fee isn't "1%" — it's ~365%/year.
- **The total cost of credit** — principal + all interest + all fees over the full term. This is the actual price you'll pay.
- **Fees beyond interest** — facility/processing fees, insurance, late penalties. These can dwarf the headline rate.
- **The repayment schedule** — how much, how often, for how long, and what happens if you're late.
- **Reducing vs flat interest** — "flat" interest (charged on the original amount the whole term) is *more expensive* than the same rate on a "reducing" balance. SACCOs typically use reducing; some lenders quote flat to look cheap.

If a lender won't clearly show you the total cost, that's a red flag.

## The rules that protect you

- **Digital lenders must be licensed.** The CBK now licenses **Digital Credit Providers (DCPs)** — only borrow from licensed ones. Unlicensed apps have abused borrowers (harassment, illegal data use). Check the lender is on the CBK's list.
- **CRB listing has rules.** Lenders report to Credit Reference Bureaus, but there are protections (e.g. limits on listing for very small amounts). You're entitled to your own credit report (Series 2, Module 8).
- **Data protection.** Lenders can't legally misuse your contacts/data — the ODPC oversees this. Predatory "shame your contacts" tactics are illegal.

Knowing your rights changes the power balance with aggressive lenders.

## How to escape a loan cycle

If you're trapped — borrowing to repay, every month — here's the way out:
1. **Stop new borrowing.** You can't fill a hole while still digging. Cut the apps off (uninstall them).
2. **List every debt** with its balance, real APR, and monthly cost. Seeing it all is half the battle.
3. **Attack the most expensive first** (usually mobile loans at ~395%), paying minimums on the rest — this saves the most money fastest.
4. **Replace expensive debt with cheap.** Where possible, take one cheaper SACCO or bank loan to clear several brutal app loans, then kill that one steadily. (Consolidation only works if you *stop* re-borrowing.)
5. **Build a small buffer** so the next shock doesn't send you back to the apps.
6. **Cut, sell, earn** to free up money for repayment — temporarily, until you're clear.

It takes discipline, but people escape these cycles every day by following exactly these steps.

## Key takeaways

- **Where you borrow is everything** — SACCO (~1%/mo) and bank loans crush mobile loans (~395%/yr) on cost.
- Read the **APR, total cost of credit, all fees, the schedule, and reducing-vs-flat** before borrowing.
- Only use **licensed Digital Credit Providers**; know your CRB and data-protection rights.
- Escape a cycle by **stopping new borrowing, listing debts, attacking the costliest first, replacing expensive with cheap, and building a buffer.**
