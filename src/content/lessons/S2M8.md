# Building & Protecting Your Credit Reputation

Your credit reputation is a financial asset you can't see — but lenders, and sometimes employers, can. Built well, it unlocks loans, a mortgage, a SACCO facility, even some jobs. Damaged, it can block all of those for years. Most Kenyans only think about the **CRB** when they're refused a loan or asked for a "clearance certificate" — by then it's a problem. This lesson explains how the system actually works, how to keep your reputation clean, and how to fix it if it's damaged (without falling for the scams that prey on the desperate).

## What the CRB system is

A **Credit Reference Bureau (CRB)** collects information about how people and businesses repay debt, and shares it with lenders so they can decide who to lend to and on what terms. In Kenya there are **three licensed CRBs**, all regulated by the CBK:
- **TransUnion**
- **Metropol**
- **Creditinfo**

When you take and repay (or default on) a loan from a regulated lender, that behaviour is reported to the CRBs and becomes part of your **credit history** — a track record that follows you.

## Your credit score

The CRBs don't just say "good" or "bad" — they produce a **credit score** that summarises your reliability as a borrower. Metropol's score (Crystalball), for example, runs from **200 to 900**, with **600 and above considered good**. A higher score signals you repay reliably, which means:
- Easier loan approvals,
- Better rates and higher limits,
- More financial doors open.

A good credit reputation isn't about *avoiding* debt entirely — it's about **handling debt responsibly**: borrowing sensibly and repaying on time builds a strong score, which is itself valuable.

## How listing (and clearance) actually works

A common myth is that the CRB is just a "blacklist." It's really a *full history* — positive and negative. But a **negative listing** (a default) is what hurts:
- When you **default** on a regulated loan, the lender reports it; your status becomes "non-performing."
- That negative record can **block future credit** and stays on your report for **5 years from the date you settle** the debt. If you *never* settle, it stays **indefinitely**.

**Clearing a listing — and this is crucial — is FREE:**
1. **Pay the outstanding debt to the original lender.**
2. The lender must **update your status to "settled" within 30 days** — this is free and required by law.
3. If they don't, **file a dispute directly with the CRB** — also free.

After settling, your status changes from "non-performing" to "settled," though the record remains for 5 years. There is **no legitimate fee to "clear your name."**

## The CRB scam to avoid

Because being listed is stressful, scammers exploit it. **Anyone who charges you a fee to "clear you from CRB" or "remove your name" is a fraudster.** Clearing a listing costs nothing — you just pay the actual debt and the lender updates your status. The only legitimate paid item is a **CRB clearance certificate** (an official document confirming your status, costing about **KES 2,200**) — which some employers or tenders ask for. Never pay anyone a "clearance fee" beyond the official certificate; it's a classic con.

## How to check your status

You have a legal right to know what's on your record:
- **Every Kenyan is entitled to one free credit report per year from each CRB** (mandated by the CBK).
- Request it from TransUnion, Metropol, or Creditinfo (online/app/USSD).
- **Check it periodically** — to confirm it's accurate, catch any errors (which you can dispute, free), and know where you stand before applying for credit.

Knowing your status *before* a lender checks it puts you in control.

## How to build and protect a good reputation

- **Repay every loan on time** — the single biggest factor. Even small mobile loans count.
- **Don't over-borrow** — keep debt manageable so you never miss payments (Series 2, Module 5).
- **Avoid defaulting on the small stuff** — a tiny unpaid app loan or a HELB default can list you (Series 5, Module 5).
- **Keep an emergency fund** so a shock doesn't force a missed payment.
- **Check your report yearly** and fix errors promptly.
- **If listed, settle it** as soon as you can — the 5-year clock starts from settlement, so the sooner you pay, the sooner it clears.

A clean credit reputation is quietly one of your most valuable financial assets — protect it.

## Key takeaways

- The **CRB** records your full credit history; three bureaus (TransUnion, Metropol, Creditinfo) are CBK-regulated.
- Your **credit score** (e.g. Metropol 200–900, good = 600+) reflects how reliably you repay — build it by **borrowing sensibly and repaying on time.**
- **Clearing a negative listing is FREE** — pay the lender, they mark it "settled" within 30 days (or dispute with the CRB).
- **Anyone charging a "CRB clearance fee" is a scammer** — only the official certificate (~KES 2,200) costs money.
- **Check your free annual report**, fix errors, and settle any listing fast (the 5-year record starts from settlement).
