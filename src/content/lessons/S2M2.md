# Opening & Using Bank Accounts Wisely

A bank account is the most basic tool in adult money life — and most people choose theirs by accident (the bank nearest campus, the one a friend uses, the one with the flashiest advert). That casual choice then leaks money for years through fees you never notice. This lesson helps you choose on purpose and use the account so it works *for* you, not against you.

## The main account types

- **Current (transactional) account** — for everyday money in and out: salary, bills, card, mobile banking. Easy access, but usually **little or no interest**. This is a spending account, not a saving one.
- **Savings account** — meant to hold money you're not spending. Pays *some* interest (often modest) and may limit withdrawals to nudge you to leave it alone.
- **Fixed deposit account** — you lock a lump sum for a set period (e.g. 3, 6, 12 months) in exchange for a **higher, guaranteed interest rate**. You can't touch it until it matures without a penalty. Good for money you genuinely won't need for a while.

A simple rule: keep your *spending* float in a current account, but don't let savings sit there earning nothing — move it to a savings account, a fixed deposit, a SACCO, or a money market fund where it actually grows.

## Choosing a bank: look past the advert

Compare on the things that actually affect you:
- **Fees** — monthly ledger/maintenance fees, withdrawal charges, transfer fees, card fees, minimum-balance penalties. These are the silent wealth-killers.
- **Access** — branch and agent network, ATM coverage, and especially the **mobile/app** experience (most banking is now on your phone).
- **Minimum balance** — some accounts charge you if your balance drops too low; many "student" or "digital" accounts have none.
- **Integration with M-Pesa** — moving money between bank and M-Pesa cheaply matters a lot in Kenya.
- **Interest** — on savings/fixed accounts, what do you actually earn?

For a young person or first-jobber, a **zero-fee or low-fee account with a good app and no minimum balance** usually beats a "prestige" account that charges you monthly for the privilege.

## The fees that quietly drain you

Over a year, small fees add up to real money. Watch for:
- **Monthly maintenance/ledger fees** — a fixed charge just for having the account.
- **Withdrawal and transfer fees** — especially bank-to-M-Pesa and ATM withdrawals.
- **SMS/alert fees** — small per-message charges that stack up.
- **Below-minimum-balance penalties.**
- **Dormancy fees** — charged on accounts you stop using.

Ask for the bank's **tariff guide** before opening, and once a year, look at your statement and total what you paid in charges. If it's significant, switch or renegotiate. A fee you don't notice is still money you lost.

## Is your money safe? Deposit protection

If a Kenyan bank ever fails, the **Kenya Deposit Insurance Corporation (KDIC)** protects each depositor up to **KES 500,000** per bank (raised from KES 100,000 in 2020; there are proposals to lift it further toward KES 1 million). This covers over 99% of accounts. Two practical takeaways:
- For most people, your everyday money is fully protected.
- If you ever hold **more than KES 500,000** in cash, spreading it across more than one bank keeps it all within the protected limit. (Though large sums usually belong in higher-earning places anyway — see the investing series.)
- Only banks supervised by the **CBK** carry this protection — another reason to stick to licensed institutions.

## Using the account wisely

- **Automate.** Set up a standing order to move a fixed amount to savings on payday — pay yourself first, before you can spend it.
- **Separate spending from saving.** Don't let your emergency fund and your fun money live in the same account where they blur together.
- **Mind the bank–M-Pesa bridge.** Frequent small transfers between bank and M-Pesa rack up fees; batch your movements.
- **Go digital, but stay alert.** Use the app for everything, but never share your PIN or OTP — your bank will never ask for them (more on fraud in Module 10).
- **Review yearly.** Banks change tariffs; your needs change. A 10-minute annual check keeps you on the best account for you.

## Key takeaways

- Match the account to the job: **current for spending, savings/fixed for saving** — don't leave savings idle in a no-interest current account.
- Choose a bank on **fees, app quality, minimum balance and M-Pesa integration**, not adverts or prestige.
- Small fees compound — **audit your charges once a year** and switch if they're high.
- **KDIC protects KES 500,000 per depositor per bank**; spread larger cash across banks and only use CBK-licensed institutions.
- **Automate savings** and keep spending and saving in separate accounts.
