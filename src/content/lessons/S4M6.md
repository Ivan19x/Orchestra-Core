# Kenyan Taxes in Full

Tax is the one money topic almost everyone avoids until it bites — a penalty, a blocked transaction, a stressful filing season. Yet the Kenyan tax system, once mapped, is logical and manageable. Whether you're employed, hustling, running a business, or earning rent, knowing which taxes apply to *you* — and how to stay compliant — saves money, stress, and trouble with KRA. This lesson is your complete, plain-language map of Kenyan taxes.

> Figures are as of 2026; tax rates change with each Finance Act, so always confirm current rates at kra.go.ke.

## First: the KRA PIN and iTax

Everything starts with a **KRA PIN** — your tax identity, free to get on **iTax** (KRA's online portal). You need it for a job, a bank/CDS account, land, a business, and more. **iTax** is where you file returns and pay. Even if all your tax is deducted at source, you must still **file an annual return** — it's free and mandatory; failing to file attracts penalties.

## Taxes on income

### PAYE (employment)
**Pay As You Earn** is income tax on salaries, deducted by your employer (Series 2, Module 1). Monthly bands (2026): 10% up to KES 24,000, then 25%, 30%, 32.5%, and 35% at the top — with a KES 2,400/month personal relief. Remitted by the **9th** of the next month.

### Withholding tax (WHT)
Tax deducted at source on certain payments and remitted to KRA. Common rates (residents, 2026):
- **Dividends:** 5%
- **Interest:** 15% (this is the tax that trims your MMF/bank interest)
- **Professional / management / consultancy fees:** 5%
- **Royalties** and others: various
For most individuals, WHT mainly shows up as the **15% on your savings interest** and **5% on dividends** — already deducted before you receive the money.

### Rental income tax (MRI)
If you earn residential rent between about **KES 280,000 and 15 million a year**, you pay **Monthly Rental Income (MRI) tax at 7.5% of gross rent** (the Finance Bill 2026 proposes raising this to 10% — confirm the current rate), due by the **20th** of the following month. (Rent paid to a non-resident is withheld at 30%.)

## Taxes on business

### Turnover Tax (TOT)
For small businesses with turnover between **KES 1 million and 25 million a year**, **TOT is 3% of gross turnover** — a simple tax that replaces the need for complex profit accounting at that scale. Due monthly (by the 20th).

### VAT (Value Added Tax)
**VAT is 16%** on most goods and services. You must **register for VAT once your turnover exceeds KES 5 million a year.** VAT-registered businesses charge 16% to customers and remit it (less VAT they paid on inputs) by the **20th**. There's also a **2% withholding VAT** in some cases. Below the KES 5M threshold, you generally don't charge VAT.

### eTIMS (electronic invoicing)
KRA now requires businesses to issue **electronic tax invoices via eTIMS**. In practice, this means most businesses (even small ones, with simplified options) must record sales electronically so KRA can see them. If you run any business, get onto eTIMS.

## Other taxes you'll meet

- **Customs / import duty** — on imported goods (relevant if you import stock).
- **Capital Gains Tax** — on the gain when you sell property or certain assets.
- **Excise duty** — on specific goods (fuel, airtime, etc.) — usually built into the price you pay.

## Deadlines and penalties — don't get caught

KRA penalties are real and avoidable. Key dates:
- **Individual annual income tax return:** by **30 June** each year.
- **PAYE:** 9th of the following month. **VAT, TOT, MRI:** 20th. **WHT:** within ~5 working days.
- **Penalties:** late filing and late payment attract penalties *plus* interest on the unpaid amount — they stack up. Filing a **nil return** (if you had no income) is still required and avoids the late-filing penalty.

The simplest protection: **know which taxes apply to you, mark the deadlines, and file on time** — even a nil return. Compliance is far cheaper than penalties.

## Which taxes apply to *you*?

- **Employed only:** PAYE (auto-deducted) + file your annual return. WHT on any savings interest/dividends is already handled.
- **Employed + side hustle:** the above, plus declare side income; TOT/VAT if the business grows past the thresholds.
- **Self-employed / business:** TOT (1–25M turnover) or full income tax, VAT if over 5M, eTIMS, plus file annually.
- **Landlord:** MRI on residential rent in the 280k–15M band.

## Key takeaways

- Everything starts with a **KRA PIN and iTax**; you must **file annually** even if tax is deducted at source (a nil return if no income).
- Income taxes: **PAYE** (employment), **WHT** (dividends 5%, interest 15%), **MRI** on rent (7.5%, proposed 10%).
- Business taxes: **TOT 3%** (turnover 1–25M), **VAT 16%** (register above 5M turnover), and **eTIMS** invoicing.
- **Deadlines matter** (annual return 30 June; most monthly taxes by the 20th; PAYE 9th) — late filing/payment incurs penalties plus interest.
- **Know which taxes apply to you** and file on time — compliance is far cheaper than penalties.
