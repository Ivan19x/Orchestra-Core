# Risk, Return & Diversification

Beneath every investment — a SACCO, a money market fund, shares, land, a bond — lie a few universal principles that govern them all. Master these and you can evaluate *any* opportunity, see through any hype, and build a portfolio that grows your money without keeping you awake at night. Ignore them and you'll either take reckless risks or, just as harmful, take too little risk to ever get ahead. This lesson covers the three ideas at the heart of all investing: **risk, return, and diversification.**

## The risk–return trade-off

The first law of investing, and the one that protects you from most scams: **higher potential return always comes with higher risk. There is no exception.**

- **Low risk → low return.** Safe assets (a savings account, a money market fund, T-bills) protect your money but grow it slowly.
- **High risk → high *potential* return.** Riskier assets (shares, a business, some property) can grow faster — but can also lose value, sometimes badly.

This single rule is your scam detector: **anyone promising high returns with no or low risk is lying.** A "guaranteed 30% a month" is not an opportunity; it's a Ponzi scheme (Series 2, Module 10). Real investing means accepting that to earn more, you must risk more — and choosing risk *deliberately*, not blindly.

Two kinds of risk matter:
- **Losing money** (the value falls).
- **Losing to inflation** (playing *too* safe, so your money grows slower than prices — Series 1, Module 6). Both are real risks; the trick is balancing them.

## Return: understand what you're really earning

A "return" is what your money earns, but read it carefully:
- **Nominal vs real** — always subtract inflation. A 9% return with 6.7% inflation is only ~2.3% *real* growth.
- **After tax** — a 9% MMF nets ~7.6% after the 15% withholding tax; an infrastructure bond's return is tax-free.
- **After fees** — costs eat returns (broker fees, fund charges).
- **Not guaranteed** — past returns don't promise future ones, especially for shares.

Compare investments on their *real, after-tax, after-fee* return — not the flashy headline number.

## Diversification: the one free lunch

Here's the most powerful idea in investing. **Diversification** — spreading your money across different assets — reduces your risk *without* necessarily reducing your expected return. It's often called investing's "only free lunch," because you get something (lower risk) for nothing.

The logic is the old wisdom: **don't put all your eggs in one basket.** If you invest everything in one share and it crashes, you're ruined. Spread across many assets, and when some fall, others hold or rise — the winners cushion the losers, and no single failure can sink you. You smooth the ride and protect yourself from being wiped out by one bad bet.

Diversify across:
- **Asset types** — MMF, SACCO, bonds, shares, property (each behaves differently).
- **Within an asset** — several shares/sectors, not one company; several SACCOs/funds if large sums.
- **Risk levels** — a mix of safe (MMF, bonds) and growth (shares, property).

A simple diversified Kenyan portfolio might combine the safety of an **MMF and bonds**, the strong returns and cheap credit of a **SACCO**, and the growth of **shares (or a REIT)** — balanced to your situation.

## Matching risk to your goals and time

The *right* level of risk isn't the same for everyone — it depends on your **goals and time horizon**:
- **Short-term money** (needed within a year or two) → **low risk**: emergency fund, MMF, T-bills. Never put money you'll need soon into volatile assets — you might be forced to sell in a dip.
- **Long-term money** (5, 10, 20+ years away — e.g. retirement, a child's university) → you can take **more risk** (shares, property), because you have time to ride out the swings, and growth matters more over decades.
- **Your temperament** — if market swings would make you panic-sell, dial back the risk to a level you can hold through ups and downs.

The order from Series 8, Module 1 still rules: **emergency fund first (safe), then invest** the long-term money according to its horizon. Match the asset to the job, and the whole plan holds together.

## Key takeaways

- **Higher return always means higher risk** — "high return, no risk" is always a lie (your scam detector).
- Read returns as **real (after inflation), after-tax, and after-fee** — and remember they're not guaranteed.
- **Diversification is the free lunch** — spreading across assets cuts risk without cutting expected return; never bet everything on one thing.
- **Match risk to your goals and time:** short-term money stays safe; long-term money can take more risk to grow.
- Build a **balanced portfolio** (MMF/bonds + SACCO + shares/REIT) suited to your situation and temperament.
